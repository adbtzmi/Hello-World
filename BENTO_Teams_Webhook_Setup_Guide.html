🍱 BENTO Teams Webhook Setup Guide v1.0

How to create a Microsoft Teams webhook for receiving BENTO compilation notifications

Micron Technology | SSD Firmware Test Engineering

📖 Overview

🔍 What is this Guide?

This guide walks you through creating a Microsoft Teams webhook using Power Automate so that BENTO can send compilation status notifications directly to your Teams chat. When a TP package compilation completes (success or failure), BENTO will send an Adaptive Card to the configured webhook URL, which Power Automate delivers as a Teams message.


This guide covers:

  • Creating a Power Automate workflow from Microsoft Teams
  • Configuring the webhook trigger to accept external requests
  • Adding the "Post card" action to deliver Adaptive Cards
  • Saving the flow and copying the webhook URL for use in BENTO
ℹ The entire setup takes approximately 5–10 minutes. Each step includes a screenshot for reference.

✅ Prerequisites

  • Microsoft Teams desktop or web app
  • Access to the Workflows app in Teams
  • Access to Power Automate (included with most Microsoft 365 licenses)
  • Permission to create flows in your organization

🔧 Create the Workflow

Step 1 Open Workflows App

1
Open Microsoft Teams Click on the 'Apps' icon on the left menu (or the three dots ⋯) and search for 'Workflows'. Select the Workflows app.

Step 2 Build From Scratch

2
Click 'Build from scratch' In the Workflows app, look for the 'Build from scratch' button near the top right and click on it.

Step 3 Select a Trigger

3
Open the trigger dropdown You will be presented with a 'New workflow' screen. Click on the '/select a trigger' dropdown.

Step 4 Go to Power Automate

4
Select Power Automate option From the dropdown menu, click on the 'Build with Power Automate to see more triggers' option.

Step 5 Confirm Power Automate

5
Click 'Go to Power Automate' A popup will appear asking to take you to Power Automate. Click on 'Go to Power Automate'.

⚡ Configure the Trigger

Step 6 Search for Webhook

6
Search for 'webhook' In the Power Automate 'Untitled flow' screen, type 'webhook' into the 'Search connectors and triggers' bar.

Step 7 Choose Teams Webhook Trigger

7
Select the webhook trigger Under the Triggers tab, locate and select the 'When a Teams webhook request is received' trigger.

Step 8 Configure Trigger URL

8
Trigger added to your flow The trigger is added to your flow. Note that the 'HTTP POST URL' field will be automatically generated and populated after you save the flow.
ℹ Don't worry that the URL field is empty — it will be generated automatically when you save the flow in Step 16.

Step 9 Set Trigger Permissions

9
Set 'Who can trigger the flow?' to 'Anyone' Click on the 'Who can trigger the flow?' dropdown and select 'Anyone'. This allows external services like BENTO to trigger it.
⚠ Important: You must select 'Anyone' for the trigger permission. If left as the default, BENTO will not be able to send webhook requests to this flow.

📬 Add the Action

Step 10 Add a New Step

10
Click '+ New step' Click on the '+ New step' button below your trigger to add an action.

Step 11 Search for Post Card Action

11
Search for 'Post card' In the 'Choose an operation' search box, search for 'Post card' and select the 'Post card in a chat or channel' action under Microsoft Teams.

Step 12 Set 'Post as'

12
Select 'Flow bot' In the newly added action, click the 'Post as' dropdown and select 'Flow bot'.

Step 13 Set 'Post in'

13
Select 'Chat with Flow bot' Next, click the 'Post in' dropdown and select 'Chat with Flow bot' (or channel, depending on your preference).
💡 Choosing 'Chat with Flow bot' sends notifications as a private chat. If you want the whole team to see them, select 'Channel' instead and specify the target channel.

Step 14 Configure Recipient and Card

14
Set Recipient and Adaptive Card Enter the recipient's email or name in the 'Recipient' field. Click inside the 'Adaptive Card' field, select 'Add dynamic content', and choose 'Attachments' from the webhook trigger.
ℹ The 'Attachments' dynamic content comes from the webhook trigger. BENTO sends the Adaptive Card JSON as an attachment in the webhook payload.

Step 15 Apply to Each Loop

15
Automatic 'Apply to each' loop Power Automate will automatically wrap your action in an 'Apply to each' loop because 'Attachments' is an array. This is expected — do not remove it.
💡 The 'Apply to each' loop is automatically created by Power Automate. It ensures each attachment in the webhook payload is processed. This is normal behavior.

💾 Save & Get Webhook URL

Step 16 Save the Flow

16
Click 'Save' Click the 'Save' button at the top right of the screen to save your workflow. The HTTP POST URL will now be generated.
⚠ Important: The webhook URL is only generated after saving. Make sure all steps are configured correctly before saving.

Step 17 View Your Workflows

17
Find your new workflow Go back to the Workflows app in Teams. Under 'Your workflows', you will see your newly created flow. Click on it.

Step 18 Copy Webhook Link

18
Copy the webhook URL On the flow's details page, click on the 'Copy webhook link' button (highlighted in red). You will use this URL in BENTO to send notifications to this Teams workflow.
💡 Paste this webhook URL into the BENTO settings (settings.json → teams_webhook_url field) to enable Teams notifications for compilation results.

🍱 Using the Webhook in BENTO

Once you have the webhook URL, configure it in BENTO:


1
Open BENTO on your local PC
2
Go to Settings — open settings.json or use the BENTO settings UI
3
Paste the webhook URL into the teams_webhook_url field
4
Save and restart BENTO — compilation notifications will now be sent to your Teams chat

ℹ BENTO sends an Adaptive Card with compilation details including: JIRA ticket, tester hostname, environment, build status (success/failure), and timestamp.

🔧 Troubleshooting

Common issues and how to resolve them.

❓ Common Issues

❌ Webhook URL not generated
Likely cause
The flow was not saved, or there is an error in the flow configuration.
How to fix
  • Ensure all steps are properly configured (no red error indicators)
  • Click Save again and wait for the URL to appear in the trigger step
  • If the URL still doesn't appear, delete the flow and recreate it
❌ BENTO notifications not arriving in Teams
Likely cause
The webhook URL is incorrect, or the trigger permission is not set to 'Anyone'.
How to fix
  • Verify the webhook URL in settings.json matches the one from Power Automate
  • Check that 'Who can trigger the flow?' is set to 'Anyone' (see Step 9)
  • Check the flow's Run history in Power Automate for error details
❌ 'Workflows' app not found in Teams
Likely cause
The Workflows app may not be enabled for your organization.
How to fix
  • Contact your IT administrator to enable the Workflows app
  • Alternatively, go directly to make.powerautomate.com to create the flow
❌ 'Post card in a chat or channel' action not available
Likely cause
The Microsoft Teams connector may not be available in your Power Automate environment.
How to fix
  • Ensure you are signed in with your organizational account
  • Try searching for 'Microsoft Teams' in the connectors list
  • Contact your IT administrator if the connector is blocked
❌ Flow runs but card is not delivered
Likely cause
The recipient email may be incorrect, or the Adaptive Card JSON may be malformed.
How to fix
  • Check the Run history in Power Automate — click on the failed run to see error details
  • Verify the recipient email address is correct
  • Ensure the 'Attachments' dynamic content is selected in the Adaptive Card field (see Step 14)
